Exit Claude and run `claude` again. Everything resets: a new context window, a new session id, and any "allow for this session" permissions you granted are gone.

Reach for this when the setup itself has to change, most often because you are moving to a different project directory. It is also the honest choice when a session has become more noise than signal, a failed approach plus an abandoned tangent, and you would rather re-state the goal cleanly than have compaction preserve the mess in summary form.

Nothing is lost. The old session is saved and remains resumable with `claude --resume`.
