One-line descriptions of the skills available, so Claude knows what it can
invoke. The skills themselves stay out of context until one is actually used.

This is progressive disclosure, and it is why a skill library scales where a
`CLAUDE.md` of the same content would not: you pay for the index, not the
shelf. Installing ten more skills adds ten more lines here, not ten more
procedures.

Two details worth knowing. A skill marked `disable-model-invocation: true` is not
in this list at all and costs nothing until you invoke it by name. And this
listing is the one piece of startup that does **not** come back after `/compact`:
only the skills you actually invoked survive.
