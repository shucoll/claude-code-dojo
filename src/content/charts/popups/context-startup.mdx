Everything Claude loads before you type: its system prompt, your environment (working directory, platform, git state), your `CLAUDE.md` files, saved memory, and the names of available skills and MCP tools.

It is small, typically under 10,000 tokens, and you never see it. It is also the only part of the window that **reloads automatically after compaction**, which is exactly why standing rules belong in `CLAUDE.md` rather than in a message you sent an hour ago.

One exception: the skill descriptions do not reload. Only skills you actually invoked survive.
